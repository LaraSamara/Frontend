Planetary research today is a strongly multidisciplinary endeavor with efforts coming from engineering and natural sciences. This book provides an up-to-date interdisciplinary geoscience focused overview of solid solar system bodies and their evolution, based on the comparative description of processes acting on them. In writing this textbook the author's objective was to provide students with a non-trivial, reasonably priced introduction to astronomy. Starting with problems astronomers face on Earth connected with observation, the book then moves on to cover the Solar System, galaxies and finally cosmology.
